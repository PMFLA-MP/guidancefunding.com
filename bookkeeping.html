Bookkeeping Services.

Clean books are the difference between a loan approval and a decline. We handle monthly bookkeeping, cleanup work, and financial reporting — so your numbers are always investor-, lender-, and IRS-ready.

Apply Now Talk to a specialist
What it is

Accurate books unlock everything.

Messy books don't just cost you time at tax season — they cost you financing opportunities. Lenders decline or price higher when they can't trust the numbers. Investors walk away. Tax prep costs balloon. Worst of all, you make operating decisions blind to what's actually happening in your business.

We keep your books clean on a monthly cadence: reconciling accounts, categorizing transactions, producing P&L and balance sheet, and flagging anything unusual. For businesses with neglected books, we also do catch-up and cleanup work to get current.

Good to know

Things to know about bookkeeping.

A few realities of small-business bookkeeping that aren't always obvious.

Cleanup ≠ ongoing

Separate pricing

Getting books current (cleanup) is priced separately from monthly bookkeeping. Cleanup is usually a one-time project; ongoing is a monthly subscription.

DIY costs more than you think

In hours and mistakes

The owner doing their own books typically spends 10–20 hours a month they could spend selling. Plus the cost of errors, missed deductions, and lender-unfriendly reports.

Software alone isn't enough

QuickBooks ≠ clean books

QuickBooks categorizes transactions using rules — and the rules are only as good as the setup. Automated books without human review are often wrong in expensive ways.

Cash vs accrual

The method matters

Most small businesses are on cash basis (simpler). Growing or inventory-heavy businesses usually need accrual (more accurate). We'll recommend the right method for your situation.

Lender-ready format

It's not just accuracy

Lenders want specific reports formatted in specific ways. We keep books in the format lenders expect, so you're never scrambling to reformat when applying.

Monthly, not annual

The #1 mistake

Bookkeeping once a year at tax time is the most common and costliest bookkeeping mistake. Monthly close prevents problems; annual review discovers them after it's too late.

Frequently asked

Common questions.

How much does bookkeeping cost? +
For most small businesses, $250–$800/month depending on transaction volume and complexity. We'll give you a fixed monthly quote after reviewing your business — no surprises.
How long does a cleanup take? +
Depends on how far behind you are and how tangled the records are. A typical 12-month cleanup for a small business takes 2–4 weeks. Heavily tangled situations can take 6–8 weeks.
Do you prepare tax returns? +
We don't prepare returns ourselves — we coordinate with a CPA. Keeping bookkeeping and tax prep separate is best practice (different skill sets, clear audit trail). If you don't have a CPA, we'll connect you with one we trust.
What software do you use? +
Primarily QuickBooks Online, though we also work with Xero and Wave. If you're on a different system, we'll assess compatibility and recommend a migration plan if needed.
Will you communicate with my vendors or customers? +
Only if you want us to. Many clients just have us handle internal books; others bring us in for A/P (paying vendors) and A/R (collecting from customers) as add-on services.
