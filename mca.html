Merchant Cash Advance.

Quick access to working capital based on your future credit card sales. Fast approval, flexible repayment tied to your daily revenue, and funding in as little as one business day.

What it is

Borrow against future earnings — get that capital today.

A merchant cash advance isn't a loan in the traditional sense. It's a purchase of a portion of your future receivables at a discount. We advance you capital now, and you repay through a small, predictable slice of your daily revenue until the agreed amount is satisfied.

That structure is what makes MCAs a great fit for businesses with strong sales but imperfect credit, short operating history, or unpredictable cash flow — the ones traditional lenders pass over most often.

How it works

Terms designed around your revenue.

An MCA adapts to the shape of your business — repayment scales with sales, not against a fixed calendar.

Fixed factor rate

Factor rate range: 15% – 49%. The advance is a fixed-fee structure, not an APR, so you know the total repayment from day one.

Flexible payment frequency

Pay back daily, weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly — whatever matches how your revenue actually comes in.

Two repayment options

ACH debits pulled from your business bank, or holdbacks taken as a percentage of each credit card batch — your call.

Fast funding

Approved in hours, funded in as little as one business day once the paperwork is in. No waiting weeks for a decision.

Scales with growth

Once 50% of your advance is paid down, you're eligible for additional funding — no reapplying from scratch.

Consolidation available

Already have advances out with other funders? We can consolidate existing MCAs to lower payments and free up daily cash flow.

Pricing factors

Rates are based on.

Your factor rate and terms are calibrated to the profile of your business. Stronger on any of these = better pricing.

01

Average monthly revenue

Higher monthly revenue typically unlocks better rates and larger advance amounts.

02

Time in business

Longer operating history signals stability — established businesses receive more favorable terms.

03

Credit profile

Personal and business credit scores influence pricing. We work with imperfect credit — it just changes the rate.

04

Financial health

Consistent deposits, low negative-day counts, and profitability all earn better offers.

05

Industry

Certain industries are lower-risk and receive preferential pricing. We price every deal individually.

Ask yourself.

  • Do you have any existing debt on your balance sheet? If yes, what are the balances and who are they owed to?
  • How do you plan on using the funding — growth, inventory, payroll, equipment, or bridging a gap?

Frequently asked

MCA questions.

What's the difference between an MCA and a traditional loan? +
A loan is a debt instrument with an APR, fixed payments, and a defined term. An MCA is a purchase of future receivables — we advance capital today in exchange for a slice of tomorrow's sales. Repayment scales with revenue, there's no compounding interest, and the total payback amount is fixed upfront by the factor rate.
How is a factor rate different from an interest rate? +
A factor rate is a flat multiplier. A 1.30 factor on a $100,000 advance means you repay $130,000 total — regardless of how long it takes. An APR compounds over time; a factor rate doesn't. Paying faster doesn't reduce the payback; paying slower doesn't increase it.
Can I qualify with bad credit? +
Yes. Credit is a pricing factor, not a hard gate. We've funded businesses with FICOs in the 500s. What matters more is revenue consistency, time in business, and bank account health. Low credit usually means a higher factor rate or a shorter initial term, not an automatic decline.
Can I get an MCA if I already have one with another funder? +
In most cases, yes. We can either stack a second position or consolidate your existing advance into a new one with lower combined payments. The consolidation route typically improves daily cash flow because the new payment replaces multiple older ones.
How fast is funding in practice? +
On clean files, we often approve within hours of receiving statements and the application, with funding the same day or next business day. More complex deals (larger amounts, consolidation, multiple bank accounts) take a bit longer — usually 2–3 business days end to end.
Are there prepayment penalties? +
Because the payback amount is fixed by the factor rate, paying early doesn't save you interest (there isn't any to save). Some funders offer a discount for early payoff — it depends on the specific program. We'll always tell you what your early-payoff number looks like before you sign.
Will an MCA show on my personal or business credit report? +
MCAs generally aren't reported to personal or business credit bureaus, so they don't build (or hurt) your credit directly. We pull credit during underwriting, but the advance itself isn't a tradeline that shows up on a credit report.
