Purchase Order Financing.

You have a confirmed order from a creditworthy buyer but lack cash to pay suppliers. P.O. financing advances the supplier cost so you can fulfill the order — then gets repaid from the buyer's payment.

Apply Now Talk to a specialist
What it is

Fulfill big orders without big cash.

Purchase order financing is a short-term, transaction-specific funding product. A lender pays your supplier directly for the goods, your supplier ships to the buyer, and once the buyer pays, the lender gets repaid — the difference is your profit.

P.O. financing is purpose-built for product-based businesses that land orders bigger than they can fund from cash on hand. Common for importers, distributors, manufacturers, wholesalers, and government contractors.

At a glance

  • AdvanceUp to 100% of supplier cost
  • Fee1.5–6% per month
  • Term30–90 days typical
  • Minimum PO size$50K+
  • Speed to fund3–10 business days
  • Industry fitImporters, distributors, mfg.
How it works

Your buyer funds the deal.

Because the advance is tied to a specific transaction with a creditworthy buyer, approval is based on the deal — not just your balance sheet.

Transaction-specific

Each P.O. is funded individually. You're not opening a facility; you're funding a specific order.

Your buyer is the risk

P.O. lenders underwrite the buyer's creditworthiness as much as yours. A strong buyer helps weaker businesses qualify.

Direct supplier payment

The lender pays your supplier directly — you never handle the cash, eliminating the risk of misallocation.

Up to 100% of cost

Most P.O. lenders will advance the entire cost of goods sold. You need little or no cash in the deal.

Short-term by design

Typical advances are 30–90 days — through the cycle of supplier payment, production, delivery, and buyer invoice payment.

Scales with sales

As your orders grow, your P.O. facility can scale with them. No reapplication for each new deal.

To apply

Required documents.

  • Signed purchase order from your buyer
  • Supplier invoice or proforma invoice
  • Completed P.O. finance application
  • Business and personal tax returns (last 2 years)
  • Bank statements (last 3–6 months)
  • Credit information on your buyer
Before you apply

Ask yourself.

  • Is the buyer creditworthy? A large, well-known buyer makes approval easier than an unknown one.
  • What's your gross margin on the order? P.O. fees eat into margin — know your math.
  • Is there flexibility in the delivery timeline? Tight timelines leave no room for supplier delays.
Good to know

Things to know about P.O. financing.

P.O. financing is powerful but specific — it doesn't fit every scenario.

Margin matters

Need 20%+ gross margin

P.O. financing fees of 3–6%/month consume margin fast. Below 20% gross margin, it's usually not economical.

Goods only

Not for services

P.O. financing is for tangible goods with clear delivery milestones. Service contracts typically don't qualify.

Buyer credit

Your buyer's credit matters

A Fortune 500 buyer helps a small business qualify. A small-business buyer with weak credit can kill the deal.

Not for startups

Product experience needed

Lenders want to see you've successfully delivered similar orders before. Completely new to the product category is a red flag.

Verified suppliers

No related-party transactions

The supplier has to be a verified third party. Lenders won't fund orders where you're effectively paying yourself.

Combine with factoring

End-to-end financing

P.O. financing pays your supplier; factoring advances you cash when you invoice. Using both lets you run with near-zero working capital.

Frequently asked

Common questions.

How is P.O. financing different from factoring? +
P.O. financing funds you before you deliver — it pays your supplier to produce the goods. Factoring funds you after you deliver — it advances against your invoice. Many businesses use both in sequence on the same deal.
What industries is P.O. financing best for? +
Importers, distributors, wholesalers, manufacturers, and government contractors. Anyone who receives confirmed orders that require significant upfront supplier payment.
What happens if my buyer doesn't pay? +
This is why buyer creditworthiness matters. Most P.O. lenders require credit insurance on the transaction or will only fund orders with insured-grade buyers. If the buyer defaults, the insurance (or the lender's reserve) covers the loss.
Can I get P.O. financing with bad credit? +
Sometimes. Because the lender underwrites the buyer as much as you, strong buyers can compensate for weaker borrower credit — but you'll still need to demonstrate the operational capability to deliver.
How fast is funding? +
Once all documents are in and the buyer is verified, funding is typically 3–10 business days. First-time deals take longer; repeat deals with the same lender can fund in 48 hours.
