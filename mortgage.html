What it is

Financing for real estate.

Commercial mortgages finance real estate used for business purposes — office buildings, warehouses, retail centers, mixed-use, hospitality, multifamily, and owner-occupied industrial. Whether you're buying a building for your own business to operate in, or acquiring investment property, we source terms across dozens of lenders.

Because real estate is high-value, durable collateral, commercial mortgages typically carry the lowest rates and longest terms of any business financing product — but the underwriting is the most rigorous.

What we finance

Real estate built for business.

Owner-occupied, investment, and mixed-use commercial properties across most asset classes.

Owner-occupied

Buy or refinance a building where your business operates. Often qualifies for SBA 504 programs at below-market rates.

Investment properties

Multifamily, office, retail, or industrial properties held for income. DSCR (debt service coverage ratio) products don't require personal income verification.

Refinance

Lower your rate, pull cash out for growth or improvements, or consolidate multiple mortgages into one.

Cash-out refinance

Use equity in owned property to fund other business initiatives — expansion, equipment, acquisitions, or working capital.

Bridge loans

Short-term financing (6–24 months) for properties being repositioned, renovated, or transitioning between permanent loans.

Construction loans

Ground-up or major renovation financing. Interest-only during construction, converts to permanent at completion.

Pricing factors

Your rate is based on.

Commercial mortgages are priced on a combination of borrower strength, property quality, and loan structure.

01

Loan-to-value (LTV)

Lower LTV (more equity in the deal) = lower rate. 65% LTV gets better pricing than 80%.

02

Debt service coverage

DSCR measures income relative to debt payment. 1.25x or higher is typical for best rates.

03

Property type & condition

Class A office or multifamily priced better than specialty or secondary-market properties.

04

Borrower experience

Experienced commercial real estate borrowers receive more favorable terms.

05

Loan structure

Fixed-rate vs ARM, recourse vs non-recourse, term length — all affect rate.

To apply

Required documents.

  • Property details (address, type, current use, NOI if income-producing)
  • Purchase contract or existing loan payoff info (for refi)
  • 2 years of business tax returns
  • 2 years of personal tax returns for owners with 20%+ equity
  • Personal financial statement and schedule of real estate owned
  • For income property: current rent roll and trailing 12-month operating statement
  • Property appraisal and environmental report (lender-ordered post-application)
Before you apply

Ask yourself.

  • Is the property owner-occupied (you'll run your business from it) or investment-only?
  • What's your target closing timeline? SBA 504 products take longer but offer better rates.
  • Are you willing to sign a personal guarantee, or do you need non-recourse financing?
Good to know

Things to understand about commercial mortgages.

Commercial real estate lending is more complex than residential — here's what matters.

Timeline

Budget 45–90 days to close

Appraisals, environmental reports, and title work take time. Start early and have backup contingencies in purchase contracts.

Recourse

Most commercial mortgages are recourse

You'll typically sign a personal guarantee. Non-recourse is available but usually requires 65% or lower LTV and strong DSCR.

Amortization vs term

They're different numbers

A common structure: 25-year amortization with a 10-year term. Your payment is calculated on 25 years, but the loan balloons at year 10 — you refinance or pay it off.

SBA 504

Up to 90% financing

For owner-occupied properties, the SBA 504 program combines a bank first mortgage, SBA second, and 10% borrower equity — lower down payment than conventional.

Prepayment

Usually has penalties

Commercial mortgages typically have prepayment penalties in the first 3–5 years — yield maintenance, defeasance, or step-down. Know your terms before closing.

DSCR products

No tax returns required

Investment property DSCR loans qualify you based on the property's income, not your personal tax returns. Great for self-employed borrowers with variable income.

Frequently asked

Common questions.

What's the minimum down payment? +
Typical conventional commercial mortgages require 20–30% down. SBA 504 can go as low as 10% for owner-occupied. DSCR investment loans often require 20–25% down.
How is a commercial mortgage different from a residential one? +
Shorter terms (usually 5, 7, or 10 years) with longer amortization (20–30 years). Balloon payments at the end of term. Recourse (personal guarantee). Commercial appraisal and more complex underwriting. Rates priced on property income and borrower strength.
Can I finance commercial real estate through an LLC? +
Yes — that's standard. You'll sign a personal guarantee, but the loan is typically in the LLC's name and the property is held in the LLC.
What's a DSCR loan? +
Debt Service Coverage Ratio. An investment property loan that qualifies you based on whether the property's rental income covers the mortgage payment (usually 1.25x or higher). No personal income docs required.
What if I have existing loans on other properties? +
We'll need a schedule of real estate owned and the debt on each. Strong borrowers with multiple properties and good cash flow often get the best terms — lenders love track records.
