What it is

Payroll without the headache.

Running payroll is deceptively complex. Federal withholdings, state income tax, unemployment insurance, workers' comp, garnishments, multi-state employees, contractor 1099s, W-2s, quarterly filings — miss any one of them and the penalties add up fast. The IRS alone assesses over $5 billion in payroll penalties each year.

We handle all of it. You tell us who earned what, we handle the calculations, tax filings, pay stubs, direct deposit, and compliance. When something changes — a new hire, a raise, a state move — we update the system and the filings adjust automatically.

Pricing factors

What affects monthly cost.

Payroll pricing is usually a base fee plus a per-employee charge. A few things move the price.

01

Employee count

The biggest factor. Most payroll pricing scales per-employee, with discounts above 25 or 50 employees.

02

Pay frequency

Weekly is more expensive to run than bi-weekly. Bi-weekly is the most common (and most cost-effective) frequency.

03

Add-on services

Benefits admin, HR support, 401k integration, workers' comp pay-as-you-go — all optional add-ons.

04

States operated in

Multi-state adds some complexity and cost — each state has its own filings and rules.

05

Contractors vs employees

1099 contractors cost less per-head to run than W-2 employees. Most platforms handle both in one account.

Good to know

Things to know about payroll.

A few realities about small-business payroll that catch first-time owners by surprise.

Penalties compound

The IRS is unforgiving

Late or incorrect federal payroll tax deposits start at 2% penalty and climb to 15%. Plus state penalties. Plus interest. A single miss can cost thousands.

Multi-state is tricky

Common DIY failure

If you have employees in more than one state, you need to withhold and file in each. Remote work has multiplied multi-state payroll errors by 10x since 2020.

1099 vs W-2

Get this right

Classifying a W-2 employee as a 1099 contractor to save on taxes is a massive risk. The IRS and state agencies are actively auditing this. We help you classify correctly.

Workers' comp

Required in most states

Most states require workers' comp for even a single W-2 employee. Pay-as-you-go workers' comp (priced on actual payroll, not estimated) is almost always better than traditional upfront policies.

Year-end matters

W-2s and 1099s due January 31

Missing January 31 filing deadlines triggers automatic penalties. We handle year-end automatically — no last-minute scramble.

HR beyond payroll

Compliance is a real risk

Employee handbooks, posted labor law notices, harassment training (required in CA, NY, IL, and others) — small businesses get fined for things they didn't know existed. We help you stay compliant.

Frequently asked

Common questions.

How much does payroll cost? +
Typical small business payroll costs $40–$80 base fee per pay run plus $6–$12 per employee. For 10 employees on bi-weekly, that's roughly $100–$200 per pay run. Add-ons (benefits admin, HR support, workers' comp) are extra.
Can you handle contractors as well as employees? +
Yes. Same system handles both W-2 employees and 1099 contractors, including year-end W-2 and 1099-NEC filings. We help you classify workers correctly, which is critical for tax and legal compliance.
How fast can you get me set up? +
Typical setup is 3–5 business days from when you send us your employee and business information. If you're migrating from an existing provider, we'll coordinate the transition so there's no gap in payroll.
What happens if there's a tax filing error? +
Our service includes tax penalty protection — if we make the error, we pay the penalty. That's standard across reputable full-service payroll providers. DIY payroll has no such protection.
Can employees access their own pay stubs and W-2s? +
Yes. Every employee gets access to a self-service portal where they can view pay stubs, download W-2s, update direct deposit info, change withholding, and manage benefits elections. This reduces HR questions dramatically.
