Cost factors

What affects your processing rate.

Your effective rate depends on the mix of transactions you process. We'll analyze your current statements to benchmark.

01

Card type mix

Credit cards cost more than debit. Rewards cards cost more than standard cards. Your specific mix drives your blended rate.

02

Ticket size

High-ticket, low-volume businesses benefit most from interchange-plus. Low-ticket, high-volume may prefer flat-rate.

03

Transaction method

Card-present (swipe/chip) is cheapest. Keyed, online, and phone transactions cost more due to fraud risk.

04

Industry

High-risk industries (certain retail categories, CBD, travel) price differently. We work with both standard and high-risk categories.

05

Monthly volume

Higher volume gets better markup pricing. Most businesses benefit from renegotiation after reaching $50K/month.

Good to know

Things to know about card processing.

Processing is a notoriously opaque industry. Here's what we wish every small business knew.

Effective rate

The one number that matters

Add up all your processing fees in a month and divide by your gross sales. That's your effective rate. Most flat-rate processors hide behind a 2.65% per-transaction quote while charging 3.5%+ effective.

"Free" terminals

Watch for locked pricing

Offers of free terminals often come with multi-year locked-in pricing. The terminal costs $300. The locked pricing costs you thousands.

Batch timing

Settle daily

Batching at end of day ensures next-day funding. Missing the batch cutoff means waiting an extra day. Small detail, meaningful cash flow impact.

Chargebacks

Protect yourself early

Keep signed receipts, delivery confirmations, and clear terms. Chargebacks cost money and hurt your processor relationship if they pile up.

Statement analysis

Free for new clients

We'll analyze your current statements line by line and show you what you're actually paying — regardless of whether you switch to us.

No-cost programs

Surcharging & cash discount

Legal in most states: pass credit card fees to customers who choose to pay by card. Can eliminate processing cost entirely. We'll help you implement compliantly.

Frequently asked

Common questions.

How much can I actually save by switching processors? +
Most businesses on flat-rate or tiered pricing save 0.3–1.0% on their effective rate by switching to interchange-plus. On $500K in annual card volume, that's $1,500–$5,000/year.
What's the difference between interchange-plus and flat-rate? +
Flat-rate pricing (like Square or Stripe) charges one rate for everything — simple but expensive. Interchange-plus breaks out the actual network cost (interchange) and adds a transparent markup. Interchange-plus is almost always cheaper above $15K/month in processing volume.
Can I keep my current equipment if I switch? +
Sometimes. It depends on whether your equipment is locked to your current processor. We'll check compatibility; if your current gear can be reprogrammed, we'll do it. If not, we offer free or discounted replacement.
How long does it take to get set up? +
New merchant accounts are typically approved in 2–5 business days. Hardware ships overnight. Full setup from application to first transaction usually takes under a week.
What if I'm in a high-risk industry? +
We work with both standard and high-risk processors. High-risk categories (certain retail, CBD, subscription billing, travel) require specialized processors but we have partners for most legal verticals.
