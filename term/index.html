Business Term Loan.

A lump sum of capital with fixed monthly payments and a defined payoff date. The classic tool for major growth investments — expansion, acquisitions, equipment, or debt consolidation.

Apply Now Talk to a specialist
What it is

Predictable capital for big moves.

A term loan is exactly what the name says: a loan with a fixed term. You borrow a lump sum upfront, then repay it in equal monthly installments over an agreed period — typically 1 to 10 years.

Because the rate and payment are fixed, term loans are ideal when you know the size of your funding need and want predictable monthly cash flow. They're the workhorse of business lending.

How it works

Straightforward, predictable financing.

No surprises, no variable rates, no balloon payments. The same amount due every month until it's paid off.

Lump sum upfront

Receive the full loan amount at close. Use it for any legitimate business purpose — no restrictions on what you spend it on.

Fixed monthly payments

Predictable cash flow. Same payment every month, no rate resets, no balloon at the end.

Competitive fixed rates

Rates start around 7% APR for strong credit. Lower than bridge loans, credit cards, or most alternative products.

Builds business credit

Reporting lenders means every on-time payment strengthens your business credit profile.

Larger amounts available

Up to $5M for qualified borrowers. Great for major expansion, real estate, or business acquisitions.

Early payoff flexibility

Many term loans allow early payoff without penalty, saving you interest costs.

Pricing factors

Your rate is based on.

Term loans reward strength — better metrics mean lower rates and longer terms.

01

Average monthly revenue

Higher monthly revenue unlocks better rates and larger funding amounts.

02

Time in business

Longer operating history signals stability — established businesses get more favorable terms.

03

Credit profile

Personal and business credit scores influence pricing. We work with imperfect credit.

04

Financial health

Consistent deposits, low negative-day counts, and profitability all earn better offers.

05

Industry

Certain industries are lower-risk and receive preferential pricing.

To apply

Required documents.

  • One-page data form (our short application)
  • 3–6 months of business bank statements
  • Last 2 years of business tax returns
  • Last 2 years of personal tax returns (for owners with 20%+ equity)
  • P&L and balance sheet (current + prior year-end)
  • Driver's license and voided business check
Before you apply

Ask yourself.

  • Do you need the full lump sum upfront, or could a line of credit give you more flexibility?
  • Is your monthly cash flow strong enough to support a fixed payment for the next several years?
  • What's the total cost of capital you're comfortable with — and does this beat your alternatives?
Good to know

Things to consider with a term loan.

Term loans are usually the cheapest option — but also the most underwriting-heavy.

Documentation

More paperwork than bridge loans

Term loans require tax returns, financials, and stronger documentation. Budget 5–10 days for underwriting.

Fixed = safe

No rate surprises

Your payment and rate are locked in. Even if interest rates rise over the life of the loan, yours doesn't change.

Prepayment

Often no penalty

Most lenders we work with allow early payoff without penalty. Some have a modest fee in year 1.

Use of funds

Flexible — but document it

Lenders like to see a purpose. "Working capital" is fine, but "acquiring equipment X to generate $Y revenue" gets better terms.

Collateral

Usually required above $250K

Smaller term loans are often unsecured. Larger ones typically require collateral — real estate, equipment, or a UCC-1 blanket lien.

Personal guarantee

Standard requirement

Business owners with 20%+ equity typically sign a personal guarantee. This is industry-standard on almost all commercial lending.

Frequently asked

Common questions.

How does a term loan compare to a bridge loan? +
A term loan has an APR (typically 7–25%), fixed monthly payments, and a defined payoff date. A bridge loan is short-term, revenue-based, and typically uses a fixed total cost (factor rate) with daily or weekly repayment that scales with your sales. Term loans are cheaper; bridge loans are faster and more flexible on credit.
Can I get a term loan with a 600 credit score? +
It's harder at lower credit scores, but not impossible. We work with lenders who consider FICO, business performance, collateral, and time in business holistically. Below 650 usually means a higher rate or shorter term.
How long does the approval process take? +
For well-documented deals, 5–10 business days. Tax returns and financial statements are the most common delay. If you have everything ready, it can be faster.
What's the difference between SBA and conventional term loans? +
SBA loans are government-guaranteed, which lets lenders offer lower rates and longer terms — but require more paperwork and 30–90 days to close. Conventional term loans are faster and simpler, but priced a bit higher.
Are there restrictions on how I use the money? +
Lenders prefer to see business-purpose use (growth, equipment, consolidation, working capital). You generally can't use the funds for personal expenses or to pay owner distributions.
