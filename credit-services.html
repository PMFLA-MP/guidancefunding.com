What drives your score

Credit score factors.

The five factors that determine your FICO score, in order of weight.

01

Payment history (35%)

Are you paying bills on time? Even one 30-day late can drop scores 60–100 points. This is the biggest factor.

02

Credit utilization (30%)

How much of your available credit are you using? Under 10% is ideal; under 30% is the commonly-cited threshold.

03

Length of history (15%)

How long have your accounts been open? Older accounts = stronger score. Don't close old cards you don't use.

04

Credit mix (10%)

Variety matters. A mix of revolving (credit cards) and installment (loans) accounts is stronger than all one type.

05

New credit (10%)

Recent inquiries and newly opened accounts can dip scores temporarily. Avoid applying for multiple things at once.

Good to know

Things to understand about credit work.

Credit improvement is real but regulated. Here's what's legitimate and what to watch out for.

Legit & legal

Everything we do is by the book

The Fair Credit Reporting Act gives you the right to dispute inaccurate items. That's the foundation of legitimate credit work — and what we do.

No "delete everything" promises

Accurate items stay

Anyone who promises to delete accurate negative items is making a promise they can't keep. Accurate items can only be removed if they're genuinely disputable.

Timeline reality

3–6 months is normal

Score improvements take time. Bureaus have 30 days to respond to disputes, and multiple rounds are often needed. Patience beats promises of instant results.

Business & personal

They're separate systems

Business credit (D&B, Experian Business) is totally separate from personal credit. Building one doesn't automatically build the other.

Tradelines

Authorized user tradelines are legal

Being added as an authorized user on someone else's credit card is a valid score-improvement tactic — and fully legal. We work with legitimate tradeline networks.

Long-term habits

Discipline maintains progress

Credit services get you to a stronger score. Keeping it requires on-time payments, low utilization, and patience. We teach the habits along the way.

Frequently asked

Common questions.

Can you really remove negative items from my credit report? +
We can dispute items that are inaccurate, incomplete, outdated, or unverifiable. If the credit bureau can't verify the item within 30 days, it must be removed. Accurate, verifiable items can't be removed and anyone promising otherwise is not operating legitimately.
How long does it take to see score improvements? +
It depends on where you're starting and what's being addressed. Some clients see 20–40 point improvements within 60 days. Major rebuilds (multiple disputes, new tradelines, utilization fixes) typically show meaningful progress at 90–180 days.
What's the difference between business credit and personal credit? +
Personal credit (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) is tied to your SSN and individual history. Business credit (Dun & Bradstreet, Experian Business) is tied to your EIN. They're separate systems — having great personal credit doesn't automatically give you business credit.
Are authorized user tradelines legitimate? +
Yes. Adding you as an authorized user on an existing credit account is a standard, legal practice. The tradeline reports to your credit history as if it were yours, which boosts your average account age and payment history.
Will you guarantee a specific score increase? +
No reputable credit services company will guarantee a specific score increase — it's not legally possible. What we guarantee is the work: disputes filed, tradelines placed, monitoring set up, and progress reports delivered.
