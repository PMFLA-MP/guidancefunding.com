Business Line of Credit.

Revolving access to capital you can draw, repay, and reuse. Pay interest only on what you use — ideal for seasonal cash flow, unexpected expenses, or funding opportunities as they arise.

Apply Now Talk to a specialist
What it is

A safety net that doubles as a growth tool.

A line of credit isn't a lump-sum loan. It's a credit limit you can draw against whenever you need it. Pull $20K today for inventory, pay it back next month when sales come in, then draw again later for payroll — without reapplying each time.

Because interest only accrues on the balance you've drawn, a line of credit is one of the cheapest ways to keep working capital on standby. Approved businesses often use it as both an emergency fund and an opportunistic growth fund.

How it works

Capital on demand.

Draw only what you need, when you need it. Repay and redraw as many times as you want.

Revolving access

Draw against your limit, repay, and draw again. No reapplying every time you need cash.

Interest only on what you use

You're charged interest only on the outstanding balance, not your total approved limit. Unused credit is free.

No prepayment penalty

Pay down your balance anytime with no fees. Reduce interest costs by paying faster when cash flow is strong.

Flexible purpose

Use it for inventory, payroll, marketing, opportunistic buys, or bridging seasonal dips. You decide.

Builds credit history

Consistent, on-time payments help build your business credit profile for future, larger financing.

Limit grows with you

As you use and repay responsibly, many lenders will increase your credit limit over time.

To apply

Required documents.

Before you apply

Ask yourself.

  • Do you need ongoing access to capital, or a one-time lump sum? If ongoing, an LOC is usually the right tool.
  • What's the largest amount you might need to draw at once? Size your limit accordingly.
  • Are you comfortable with variable-rate interest? Most LOCs are variable-rate products.
Good to know

Worth knowing about lines of credit.

A few points people often miss before comparing an LOC to a term loan.

Unused = free

No cost for idle credit

If you never draw, you pay nothing. Some lenders charge a small annual maintenance fee; we work with ones that don't where possible.

Draw any time

24/7 access via online portal

Need funds on a Sunday night? Draw via your portal, funds hit your account within 1–2 business days.

Variable rate

Rates can change

Most LOCs are variable-rate. When prime goes up, so does your rate. When it drops, you benefit.

Collateral varies

Secured or unsecured options

Unsecured LOCs are available for strong borrowers. Larger limits often require collateral (equipment, A/R, real estate).

Renewal

Annual reviews

Most LOCs are reviewed annually. Keep payments current and financials clean and renewals are typically automatic.

Fast to draw

Funds in 1–2 business days

Once approved, draws are quick. Unlike a term loan, you don't reapply each time you need money.

Frequently asked

Common questions.

What's the difference between a line of credit and a term loan? +
A term loan is a lump sum you borrow once and pay back over a fixed schedule. A line of credit is a revolving limit you draw from as needed, paying interest only on what you use. Term loans are better for single large purchases; LOCs are better for ongoing or unpredictable capital needs.
Can I have both a line of credit and a term loan? +
Yes — many businesses use both strategically. A term loan for a major one-time expense (equipment, expansion), and an LOC for ongoing working capital and emergencies.
Will applying hurt my credit score? +
A soft inquiry during pre-qualification has no impact. A hard inquiry happens only when you formally accept terms and proceed with underwriting. We'll always tell you before triggering a hard pull.
Do I have to use the full credit limit? +
No. You can draw as little or as much as you want, up to your approved limit. Most borrowers use only 30–60% of their approved capacity on average.
What happens if I default on a draw? +
Missing payments can trigger default, accelerated repayment, and credit score damage. If you hit trouble, contact your lender early — most will work with you on a temporary modification rather than accelerate.
