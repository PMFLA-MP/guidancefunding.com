Equipment Financing.

Buy the equipment you need without tying up cash. We finance up to 100% of cost for vehicles, machinery, tech, and specialty equipment — using the asset itself as collateral.

What it is

Let the equipment pay for itself.

Equipment financing is a purpose-built loan for buying business equipment. Because the equipment serves as the collateral, rates are lower than unsecured financing, and approval is easier — even with less-than-perfect credit.

The term structure usually matches the useful life of the equipment, so the monthly payment stays well below what the equipment produces in revenue. New or used, owned outright or leased — we structure the financing around your cash flow.

How it works

The equipment is the collateral.

Because the loan is secured by the asset, terms are stronger and approval is easier than unsecured options.

Any equipment type

Vehicles, machinery, medical devices, restaurant equipment, IT hardware, construction equipment — if it's for your business, we can finance it.

Up to 100% financing

Zero down on most deals for qualified borrowers. Preserve working capital for other uses.

New or used

Finance new equipment, used equipment, or refinance equipment you already own to free up cash.

Lower rates

Because the equipment is collateral, rates are typically lower than unsecured business loans.

Flexible terms

Term matched to equipment life. A 7-year machine gets a 7-year loan. Payments stay manageable.

Easier approval

Credit requirements are more lenient than unsecured loans. Even startups can qualify with a strong down payment.

Pricing factors

Your rate is based on.

Equipment type, age, and borrower profile all factor into the rate and term offered.

01

Equipment type and age

New equipment with long useful life earns the best rates. Older or specialized equipment may require larger down payments.

02

Time in business

Longer operating history signals stability. Startups can qualify with stronger down payments or collateral.

03

Credit profile

Personal and business credit influence pricing. We work with imperfect credit — it changes rate, not eligibility.

04

Cash flow

Bank statements and P&L show whether the business can support the monthly payment comfortably.

05

Down payment

Higher down payment = lower rate and easier approval. 10–20% down usually unlocks best-tier pricing.

To apply

Required documents.

  • One-page data form (our short application)
  • 3 months of business bank statements
  • Equipment quote or invoice from the vendor
  • Driver's license and voided business check
  • For larger deals: tax returns and financial statements
Before you apply

Ask yourself.

  • Is this equipment revenue-producing, or a cost-saving purchase? The answer affects how we structure the deal.
  • What's the expected useful life of the equipment? The loan term should match.
  • Do you have a preferred vendor, or do you need us to help source competitive quotes?
Good to know

Worth knowing about equipment financing.

A few structural details that can save you money or complexity.

Section 179

Tax deduction advantage

Equipment financing often qualifies for Section 179 deductions — you can write off the full purchase in year one, even though you're paying over time.

Lease vs loan

Two structures available

An equipment loan means you own it at the end. A lease means you rent it, often with a purchase option at the end. We can structure either way.

Sale-leaseback

Unlock cash from equipment you own

Already own the equipment? We can buy it from you for cash and lease it back. Instant liquidity without giving up use.

Soft costs

Installation and training covered

Most lenders finance not just the equipment itself but also installation, training, taxes, and shipping — all rolled into one loan.

Credit-flexible

Works for newer businesses

Because the equipment is collateral, even 1-year-old businesses can qualify with a reasonable down payment.

Pre-approval

Shop with financing in hand

We can pre-approve you for a financing amount before you finalize the equipment choice — negotiate with cash in hand.

Frequently asked

Common questions.

Can I finance used equipment? +
Yes. Used equipment is typically fine, though terms may be shorter and rates slightly higher than new. Most lenders cap the term at the remaining useful life of the equipment.
What's the difference between leasing and financing? +
Financing means you own the equipment at the end — you're building equity with each payment. Leasing means you're renting; at the end, you either return it, buy it out, or renew. Leases often have lower monthly payments but no ownership.
Do I need a down payment? +
Not always. For strong credit with new equipment, zero down is common. For older equipment, startups, or weaker credit, expect 10–20% down.
Can I finance equipment from a private seller? +
Yes, but it's more work. We need the seller to complete a bill of sale and title transfer. Business-to-business private sales are easier than individual sellers.
Is equipment financing reported to credit bureaus? +
Most equipment lenders report to business credit bureaus (Dun & Bradstreet, Experian Business). On-time payments build your business credit profile.
