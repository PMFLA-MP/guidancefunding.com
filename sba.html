SBA Loan.

Government-guaranteed financing that lets lenders offer the lowest rates and longest terms in small business lending. Slower to close, but unmatched on cost of capital for eligible borrowers.

Apply Now Talk to a specialist
What it is

The gold standard of small business loans.

SBA loans aren't made by the SBA — they're made by private lenders and partially guaranteed by the Small Business Administration. That government backstop reduces the lender's risk, which means better terms for you: lower rates, longer terms, smaller down payments, and more flexible qualifying.

The trade-off is paperwork and time. SBA loans require more documentation than conventional loans and typically take 30–90 days to close. For the right borrower, the cost savings over the life of the loan are well worth it.

SBA programs

Three SBA loan programs.

Each program fits a different use case. We'll match you to the right one based on your need.

7(a) — The flagship

Up to $5M for almost any business purpose: working capital, equipment, real estate, debt refinance, acquisitions. The most flexible SBA program.

504 — Real estate & equipment

For buying commercial real estate or major equipment. 10% down, 40% SBA second mortgage, 50% bank first. Below-market fixed rate on the SBA portion.

Microloans — Up to $50K

Small-dollar loans through nonprofit intermediaries. Great for startups or very small businesses that need limited working capital.

Lowest rates available

SBA 7(a) rates are capped at prime + 2.75–4.75% — typically the lowest business loan rates in the market.

Long terms

Up to 10 years for working capital and equipment, up to 25 years for real estate. Keeps monthly payments manageable.

Lower down payments

10% down on SBA 504 real estate deals. Some 7(a) loans require no down payment at all.

Eligibility factors

What SBA lenders look for.

SBA underwriting is rigorous. Here's what strong approval files have in common.

01

Business size

Must be a "small business" by SBA standards (varies by industry; usually under 500 employees).

02

Credit score

640+ personal FICO is typical minimum. 680+ unlocks best pricing. Business credit also considered.

03

Time in business

2+ years in business is standard. Startups can qualify but need strong personal financials and experience.

04

Cash flow

The business must show it can service the debt from operating income. DSCR of 1.25x+ is typical.

05

Collateral

SBA doesn't require full collateral coverage, but lenders will take what's available — real estate, equipment, accounts receivable.

To apply

Required documents.

  • Completed SBA Form 1919 (Borrower Information Form)
  • 3 years of business tax returns
  • 3 years of personal tax returns (all owners with 20%+ equity)
  • Personal financial statement (SBA Form 413)
  • Year-to-date P&L and balance sheet
  • Business debt schedule
  • Business plan (for startups and some expansions)
  • Additional docs vary by loan type and use
Before you apply

Ask yourself.

  • Can you afford to wait 30–90 days for funding, or do you need capital sooner?
  • Is your credit score 640+ and do you have at least 2 years of business operating history?
  • Are you prepared to provide detailed tax returns, financial statements, and a personal guarantee?
Good to know

Things to know about SBA loans.

SBA loans are the best product for eligible borrowers — but the application process is not for the faint of heart.

Time

30–90 days to close

Start early. SBA loans involve the lender, the SBA, underwriting, appraisals, and legal. Budget at least 45 days, often more.

Paperwork

Extensive documentation

Tax returns, financial statements, projections, debt schedule, resumes of owners, personal financial statements — SBA wants a complete picture.

Personal guarantee

Required from all 20%+ owners

Every owner with 20% or more of the business signs a personal guarantee. Non-negotiable on SBA.

Fees

Guaranty fee built into the loan

SBA charges a one-time guaranty fee (2–3.75% depending on loan size) that's typically financed into the loan. Factor it into your cost analysis.

Refinance limits

Can't just refinance any debt

SBA has specific rules on what debts can be refinanced. Existing SBA debt, for example, generally can't be refinanced with another SBA loan.

Preferred lenders

Work with a PLP lender

Preferred Lender Program lenders can approve SBA loans without SBA review, shortening the timeline significantly. We work with PLP lenders whenever possible.

Frequently asked

Common questions.

Why choose SBA over a conventional loan? +
Lower rates, longer terms, smaller down payments, and more flexible qualifying. The trade-off is time and paperwork. Over the life of a 10-year loan, SBA usually saves borrowers tens of thousands of dollars.
What's the difference between SBA 7(a) and 504? +
7(a) is general-purpose — working capital, equipment, refinance, acquisition. 504 is specifically for buying commercial real estate or major long-life equipment. 504 has a different structure (bank + SBA + borrower) but lower fixed rates on the SBA portion.
Can I get an SBA loan to start a business? +
Yes, though it's harder. Startups need strong personal credit, industry experience, a detailed business plan, and often 20–30% cash injection. It's a higher bar than for existing businesses.
What can I use an SBA 7(a) loan for? +
Almost anything business-related: working capital, inventory, equipment, real estate, refinancing debt, buying a business, leasehold improvements, marketing, hiring. Not allowed: speculative investments, owner distributions, paying off personal debt.
How much can I actually borrow? +
7(a) caps at $5M. 504 caps at $5.5M (combined bank + SBA). Microloans cap at $50K. The actual amount you qualify for depends on cash flow, collateral, and use of funds — usually 1.25–1.5x your annual EBITDA.
