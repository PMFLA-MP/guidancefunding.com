Bridge Loan.

Fast, short-term capital to bridge cash flow gaps and capture time-sensitive opportunities. Approved in hours, funded in as little as one business day — with flexible repayment structured around your business.

What it is

Capital when timing matters.

A bridge loan is short-term financing designed to close a gap — covering payroll between receivables, funding inventory for a big order, bridging between permanent financings, or jumping on an opportunity that won't wait. Speed and flexibility are the whole point.

The product is structured to work for businesses with strong sales but imperfect credit, short operating history, or unpredictable cash flow — the ones traditional lenders pass over most often. Repayment is tied to your actual revenue, so it scales up when sales are strong and down when they're not.

How it works

Terms designed around your revenue.

A bridge loan adapts to the shape of your business — repayment scales with sales, not against a fixed calendar.

Fixed factor rate

Factor rate range: 15% – 49%. The advance is a fixed-fee structure, not an APR, so you know the total repayment from day one.

Flexible payment frequency

Pay back daily, weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly — whatever matches how your revenue actually comes in.

Two repayment options

ACH debits pulled from your business bank, or holdbacks taken as a percentage of each credit card batch — your call.

Fast funding

Approved in hours, funded in as little as one business day once the paperwork is in. No waiting weeks for a decision.

Scales with growth

Once 50% of your advance is paid down, you're eligible for additional funding — no reapplying from scratch.

Consolidation available

Already have advances or loans out with other funders? We can consolidate existing short-term debt to lower payments and free up daily cash flow.

Before you apply

Ask yourself.

  • Do you have any existing debt on your balance sheet? If yes, what are the balances and who are they owed to?
  • How do you plan on using the funding — growth, inventory, payroll, equipment, or bridging a gap?
  • How quickly do you need the funds?

Frequently asked

Bridge loan questions.

What's the difference between a bridge loan and a traditional loan? +
A traditional loan is a debt instrument with an APR, fixed monthly payments, and a multi-year term. A bridge loan is short-term — typically 30 days to 24 months — priced with a fixed total cost and repaid through a slice of your daily revenue. There's no compounding interest, and the total payback amount is fixed upfront, so you know exactly what the capital costs before you sign.
How is a factor rate different from an interest rate? +
A factor rate is a flat multiplier. A 1.30 factor on a $100,000 advance means you repay $130,000 total — regardless of how long it takes. An APR compounds over time; a factor rate doesn't. Paying faster doesn't reduce the payback; paying slower doesn't increase it.
Can I qualify with bad credit? +
Yes. Credit is a pricing factor, not a hard gate. We've funded businesses with FICOs in the 500s. What matters more is revenue consistency, time in business, and bank account health. Low credit usually means a higher factor rate or a shorter initial term, not an automatic decline.
Can I get a bridge loan if I already have other business debt? +
In most cases, yes. We can either stack a second position or consolidate your existing advance into a new one with lower combined payments. The consolidation route typically improves daily cash flow because the new payment replaces multiple older ones.
How fast is funding in practice? +
On clean files, we often approve within hours of receiving statements and the application, with funding the same day or next business day. More complex deals (larger amounts, consolidation, multiple bank accounts) take a bit longer — usually 2–3 business days end to end.
Are there prepayment penalties? +
Because the payback amount is fixed by the factor rate, paying early doesn't save you interest (there isn't any to save). Some funders offer a discount for early payoff — it depends on the specific program. We'll always tell you what your early-payoff number looks like before you sign.
Will a bridge loan show on my personal or business credit report? +
Bridge loans of this type generally aren't reported to personal or business credit bureaus, so they don't build (or hurt) your credit directly. We pull credit during underwriting, but the advance itself isn't a tradeline that shows up on a credit report.
